What is the Start, Stop, Continue Model?

The "Start, Stop, Continue" (SSC) feedback model is a concise and actionable framework for providing constructive feedback. It encourages clarity and focuses on behaviors that can be changed or reinforced. It breaks feedback into three distinct categories:

  • Start: What new actions, behaviors, or initiatives should the person or team *start* doing? These are typically suggestions for improvement or new approaches.
  • Stop: What actions, behaviors, or practices should the person or team *stop* doing? These are things that are hindering progress, creating inefficiencies, or having a negative impact.
  • Continue: What actions, behaviors, or practices should the person or team *continue* doing? These are positive things that are working well and should be sustained or amplified.

Why Use Start, Stop, Continue Feedback?

This model is highly effective for several reasons:

  • Action-Oriented: It forces both the giver and receiver of feedback to think about concrete actions rather than general feelings or criticisms.
  • Balanced Perspective: By including "Continue," it ensures that positive contributions are recognized, which can make difficult "Stop" feedback easier to receive.
  • Easy to Understand: The three clear categories make the feedback easy to comprehend and act upon.
  • Versatile: It can be used in one-on-one meetings, team retrospectives, project reviews, or even for self-reflection.

Best Practices for Effective SSC Feedback

While the model is simple, its effectiveness lies in how it's delivered and received.

  • Be Specific: Always provide concrete examples for each point. Instead of "Stop being late," try "Stop arriving 10 minutes late to stand-up meetings three times this week."
  • Timeliness: Deliver feedback as close to the event or behavior as possible.
  • Focus on Behavior, Not Personality: Frame feedback around actions that can be changed.
  • Be Open to Dialogue: Feedback should be a two-way conversation. Encourage the receiver to ask questions and share their perspective.
  • Follow Up: Check in later to discuss progress and see if the feedback led to intended changes.
