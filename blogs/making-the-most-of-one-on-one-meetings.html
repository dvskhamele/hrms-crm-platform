Making the Most of One-on-One Meetings: A Manager's Guide

One-on-one meetings are arguably the most important tool a manager has for building strong relationships with their team members, fostering development, and ensuring alignment. When conducted effectively, these meetings can significantly boost employee engagement, performance, and retention. This guide provides best practices for making your one-on-ones truly impactful.

Why Effective One-on-Ones Matter

  • Build Trust & Rapport: Dedicated time strengthens the manager-employee relationship.
  • Address Concerns Proactively: Provides a safe space for employees to raise issues before they escalate.
  • Support Development: Focuses on career growth, skill development, and goal setting.
  • Improve Performance: Offers opportunities for feedback, coaching, and performance discussions.
  • Increase Engagement: Employees feel heard, valued, and supported.

Best Practices for Managers

1. Prepare an Agenda (but be flexible)

Have a loose agenda covering key areas like performance, development, and well-being. However, prioritize the employee's topics. This is their meeting.

2. Listen More, Talk Less

Your primary role is to listen actively. Ask open-ended questions and allow the employee to lead the conversation. Aim for the employee to speak 80% of the time.

3. Focus on the Employee's Development

Discuss career aspirations, learning opportunities, and how you can support their growth. This is a key differentiator from project-focused meetings.

4. Provide Constructive Feedback

Offer specific, actionable feedback, both positive and constructive. Focus on behaviors and impact, not personality.

5. Follow Up on Action Items

Document key discussion points and agreed-upon action items. Follow up on these in subsequent meetings to show accountability and progress.
