Understanding Employee Turnover and Its Impact

Employee turnover refers to the rate at which employees leave an organization over a specific period. It can be voluntary (employees choose to leave) or involuntary (employees are terminated). While some turnover is natural and even healthy, excessive turnover can have significant negative consequences:

  • Increased Costs: Recruitment, hiring, onboarding, and training new employees are expensive.
  • Decreased Productivity: Loss of experienced staff can lead to knowledge gaps and reduced output.
  • Lower Morale: High turnover can negatively impact the morale and engagement of remaining employees.
  • Loss of Institutional Knowledge: Valuable experience and expertise walk out the door with departing employees.
  • Impact on Employer Brand: A reputation for high turnover can make it harder to attract top talent.

How Our Employee Turnover Rate Calculator Works

Calculating your turnover rate is straightforward with our tool:

  1. Enter Average Number of Employees: Input the average number of employees during the period you are analyzing (e.g., a month, quarter, or year).
  2. Enter Number of Separations: Input the total number of employees who left the organization during that same period.
  3. Calculate Turnover Rate: The tool instantly calculates the employee turnover rate as a percentage.

The formula used is: (Number of Separations / Average Number of Employees) * 100. This provides a standard metric for comparison and analysis.
