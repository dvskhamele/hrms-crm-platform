A Guide to UK Statutory Redundancy Pay

Redundancy is a difficult process for both employers and employees. Understanding the legal requirements for redundancy pay is crucial for ensuring a fair and compliant process. This guide covers the basics of statutory redundancy pay in the UK (England, Scotland, and Wales).

Who is Eligible for Statutory Redundancy Pay?

To be eligible for statutory redundancy pay, an individual must:

  • Be an employee working under a contract of employment.
  • Have at least two years of continuous service with the employer.
  • Have been dismissed due to a genuine redundancy situation.

How is Statutory Redundancy Pay Calculated?

The amount of statutory redundancy pay depends on three factors:

  • The employee's age.
  • The employee's length of service (capped at 20 years).
  • The employee's average weekly pay (capped at a government-set limit, currently Â£700 as of April 2024).

The calculation is based on a multiplier for each full year of service:

  • Age 41 and over: 1.5 weeks' pay for each year of service.
  • Age 22 to 40: 1 week's pay for each year of service.
  • Under age 22: 0.5 week's pay for each year of service.

Contractual vs. Statutory Redundancy Pay

It's important to note that this guide covers the legal minimum (statutory) redundancy pay. Some employers may offer a more generous contractual redundancy package. Always check the employment contract.
